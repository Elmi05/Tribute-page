Introduction

Dr. Maxamed Daahir Afrax was a notable Somali author and scholar whose literary contributions have left a lasting impact on Somali literature and culture. Throughout his life, Afrax's creative endeavors revolved around themes such as cultural identity, poetry, and patriotism, resonating deeply with the Somali community.

Early Life

Born in 1952, Maxamed Dahir Afrax's literary journey began at a young age. He showed a deep passion for writing and storytelling, even during his early years. Afrah started his literary work in Mogadishu in the late 1970s by publishing weekly papers and short novels in the national journal Xiddigta Oktober (October Star). In 1972, he started publishing his own journal in Somali, where he wrote about the social changes and their impact on Somali society. According to Afrah, despite social change being a way of human interaction, he was worried that the new cultural and social transformation would have a negative impact on society.

Literary Achievements

Dr. Maxamed Daahir Afrax did extraordinary work to preserve the Somali language by helping form Somali PEN and the AGA-Academy of Somali Language. Afrah was versed in three languages: English, Arabic, and Somali, as he used to write in all three languages.

Some of his popular books written in Somali, Arabic, and English include:

  1. Maana Faay
  2. Galtimacruuf
  3. Guur-ku-sheeg
  4. Dal Dad Waayay iyo Duni Damiir Beeshay
  5. Fan-masraxeedka Soomaalida
  6. Transitional Nature of Somali Post-Independence Literature
  7. The changing shape of Somali Poetry
  8. نافذة على الإبداع في منطقة خليج عدن

Legacy

Dr. Mohamed Dahir Afrah passed away on October 9, 2021, in London. The Somali language lost its champion with this sad death. However, his efforts will not go to waste, as will the work and institutions he created. Despite Afrah having passed away, he will remain in the hearts and minds of Somali history as the man whose efforts have inspired generations of Somalis.
